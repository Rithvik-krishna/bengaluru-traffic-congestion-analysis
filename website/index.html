Project Overview

Understanding Bengaluru's urban grid congestion problem

Bengaluru, known globally as the tech hub of India, has witnessed unprecedented economic growth and urbanization over the past few decades. However, this growth has come at a massive cost to its transportation infrastructure. The city suffers from chronic, severe traffic congestion that results in billions of hours lost, millions of liters of wasted fuel, and significant environmental degradation.

This analysis utilizes a large-scale, high-fidelity traffic dataset representing multi-point observations across major transit corridors and junctions in Bengaluru. By exploring historical trends, peak hours, weather effects, and roadway properties, this project aims to diagnose key pressure points and provide actionable, policy-relevant solutions.

By applying advanced data processing pipelines and exploratory statistical plotting, we trace the precise mathematical relationships between traffic volumes, average vehicle speeds, capacity utilization, and congestion levels to lay the groundwork for intelligent transport planning.

Analysis Objectives

  • Diagnose the daily temporal traffic cycles and pinpoint morning/evening peak intervals.
  • Identify the most congested bottleneck coordinates and hotspots across Bengaluru.
  • Quantify the impact of rainy weather conditions on traffic speeds and grid delays.
  • Evaluate weekday versus weekend traffic variations and calculate commute deviations.
  • Model the traffic volume-speed relationship to predict critical capacity breakdown levels.

Exploratory Data Analysis

Statistical visualizations mapping traffic dynamics

1. Traffic Volume Distribution

Observation

The traffic volume follows a right-skewed normal distribution with an average traffic rate centered around the middle of the range.

Insight

Most observations fall into a distinct interquartile range (IQR), but extreme high-traffic outliers occur frequently on specific arterial roads during commute spikes.

Recommendation

Establish dynamic arterial lanes that expand or contract based on vehicle distribution density.

2. Congestion Level Distribution

Observation

The boxplot shows a wide spread of congestion levels across all areas, with a median congestion hovering near moderate values.

Insight

A significant number of extreme high-congestion events (outliers above the upper whisker) occur, highlighting localized grids locking up completely.

Recommendation

Deploy specialized rapid-action traffic response teams to bottleneck intersection spots identified as sources of extreme lockup events.

3. Chronological Monthly Traffic Trend

Observation

Traffic volumes show distinct fluctuations month-over-month throughout the 2022–2024 time period, with specific seasonal variations.

Insight

Chronological trends capture overall traffic density growth and long-term network demand shifts. Hourly analysis could not be performed because the dataset only contains daily timestamps.

Recommendation

Use long-term trend data to plan infrastructure expansion and coordinate maintenance cycles during lower-demand seasons.

4. Traffic Volume vs Speed

Observation

The scatter plot shows a strong, non-linear negative relationship where average speed decays rapidly as volume increases.

Insight

Once traffic volume crosses a critical threshold, speed drops precipitously to less than 15 km/h, highlighting a collapse in road network capacity.

Recommendation

Implement smart ramp metering and volume control systems to prevent the traffic density from crossing the critical collapse threshold.

5. Top 10 Most Congested Areas

Observation

Congestion is highly concentrated, with a subset of areas having average congestion levels exceeding 50%.

Insight

Key IT hubs and dense commercial zones represent the highest congestion levels in the city, acting as focal points for transit delay propagation.

Recommendation

Prioritize transit-oriented development and high-capacity metro corridors directly connecting these top 10 zones.

6. Weather Impact Analysis

Observation

Rainy and adverse weather conditions correspond to a drop in average speeds, and a concurrent rise in congestion levels and travel time index.

Insight

Rain reduces road friction, impairs visibility, and decreases overall capacity, resulting in compound delay effects across the network.

Recommendation

Integrate weather-responsive traffic signal timing plans that adjust cycle lengths dynamically when rainfall is detected.

7. Traffic Volume by Day of Week

Observation

Traffic volume remains consistently high on weekdays (Monday-Friday) and drops notably on weekends (Saturday-Sunday).

Insight

The weekday-weekend disparity confirms that employee commuting is the primary driver of traffic volume in Bengaluru.

Recommendation

Encourage corporate companies to implement hybrid work policies (e.g., rotating remote work days) to flatten the weekday volume curve.

Strategic Conclusions

The data clearly demonstrates that Bengaluru's traffic congestion is not uniform. It is heavily localized around specific economic hotspots and displays sharp temporal peaks tied to corporate working hours. High volume leads to a rapid, non-linear degradation of vehicle speeds, showing that minor increases in traffic volume beyond critical capacity thresholds lead to network collapse.

Furthermore, weather acts as a major disruptor, showing that infrastructure resilience is just as critical as capacity extension. When rainfall occurs, congestion levels surge, showing that poor water drainage and lack of adaptive signalling lead to massive compound delays.

Addressing this gridlock requires moving away from adding raw road capacity (which induces more demand) and instead focusing on demand-side management, public transit integration, and smart dynamic signaling systems.

Strategic Recommendations

A phased, actionable roadmap for Bengaluru's transport grid

Short-term (0-6 months)

Tactical Interventions

  • Deploy extra traffic marshals and response teams to the top 10 hotspot junctions during peak hours.
  • Implement weather-adaptive traffic signal plans that prolong signal cycles during rainfall.
  • Launch public awareness campaigns for signal compliance and lane discipline.
Medium-term (6-12 months)

Systemic Optimizations

  • Incentivize large corporate parks to stagger office login/logout times by 1-2 hours.
  • Establish dedicated bus corridors and micro-mobility stations connecting to major metro hubs.
  • Introduce smart parking systems to reduce cruising traffic searching for parking spaces.
Long-term (1+ years)

Structural Reforms

  • Accelerate metro rail phase extensions to link high-density residential zones with IT corridors.
  • Implement congestion pricing zones in central business districts during peak times.
  • Transition to unified, AI-driven smart traffic management systems.
