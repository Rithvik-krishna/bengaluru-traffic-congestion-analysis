BENGALURU TRAFFIC CONGESTION ANALYSIS - EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

PROBLEM STATEMENT:
Bengaluru experiences severe traffic congestion impacting commute times, fuel consumption,
and environmental quality. This analysis explores daily traffic patterns, regional hotspots,
and factors like weather to help guide infrastructure strategy.

KEY FINDINGS:
1. PEAK TRAFFIC HOURS (LIMITATION): Hourly analysis could not be performed because the dataset only contains daily timestamps.
2. HOTSPOT AREAS: Top congested zones are led by Koramangala (94.0%) and M.G. Road (90.6%).
3. WEATHER IMPACT: Average congestion is 80.54% under Rainy conditions vs 80.72% under Clear conditions (a difference of -0.23%).
4. WEEKDAY vs WEEKEND: Weekday traffic averages 29297.2 vehicles, which is 0.73% higher than weekend volume of 29084.0 vehicles.
5. VOLUME-SPEED RELATIONSHIP: There is a negative correlation (-0.3411) where speed drops by ~0.28 km/h for every 1000 vehicle increase.

STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS:
Short-term (0-6 months):
- Deploy traffic personnel at hotspot junctions during expected peak commute windows
- Stagger signals dynamically during severe bottlenecks
- Conduct maintenance checks on road drainage systems

Medium-term (6-12 months):
- Incentivize workspace staggered logins to distribute daily traffic
- Establish dedicated transit corridors connecting top hotspots
- Expand park-and-ride facilities near metro lines

Long-term (1+ years):
- Install sub-daily telemetric traffic sensors to capture hourly trends
- Accelerate metro lines directly feeding into the highest congestion hotspots
- Leverage smart signaling and congestion pricing models

EXPECTED IMPACT: 10-15% delay reduction across key corridors through demand management.
